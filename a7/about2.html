Grandpa Peter Piper

The Farm that Peter Piper Built

Peter Piper built his farm in rural Illinois in 1920, just about 100 years after the fine state of Illinois was founded. Peter, a young buck at the time, walked into the Illinois land office and paid a mere $150 for the land. Little did he know, this land was to become legendary throughout the world as the premier spot of the world's finest peppers.

In the following years, Peter Piper built his home, plowed the fields and raised a family with his wife, Patricia. The farm flourished, but was otherwise unheard of until 1933.

As the story goes, every day for nearly a year during the Century of Progress, Chicago World's Fair, Peter Piper would ride his horse into town brandishing his peppers, pickled by his wife, Patricia. Fair-goers went wild for them, spreading his name and his trademark limeric throught the world.
